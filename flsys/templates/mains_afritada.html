Chicken Afritada

Afritada, also known as afritadang manok, is a Filipino chicken stew with a tomato-based sauce. It's a simple dish that can be prepared on any regular days. It's perfect for lunch, and it's even better with some warm white rice. This simple local chicken dish is likely to become a filipino family favorite. It's made with full chicken pieces, large chunks of potatoes and carrots, and a tasty tomato sauce.

From Panlasang Pinoy by Vanjo Merano
Prep. Time

10 min.

Cooking Time

45 min.

Serving

4 servings

Ingredients

1½ lbs. Chicken cut into serving pieces

2 piece potato cubed

1 piece carrot sliced

8 oz. tomato sauce

3 pieces hotdog sliced

½ cup green peas

3 pieces bay leaves

1 piece red onion chopped

2 teaspoons garlic minced

3 cups chicken broth

½ teaspoon sugar

3 tablespoons cooking oil

Salt and ground black pepper to taste

Instructions

step 1

In a cooking saucepan, heat the oil. Saute the onion and garlic in the saucepan until the onion is softened.

step 2

Add chicken. Cook for 30 seconds. Turn it over and cook the other side for another 30 seconds.

step 3

Pour tomato sauce and chicken broth. Cover. Let boil.

step 4

Add dried bay leaves. Cover the cooking pot. Continue to cook in medium heat for 30 minutes.

step 5

Add hotdogs and carrot. Cook for 3 minutes.

step 6

Add potato. Cover the pot and cook for 8 minutes.

step 7

Add green peas. Cook for 2 minutes.

step 8

Season with salt and ground black pepper

step 9

Top with grated cheddar cheese and serve hot with warm rice. Enjoy!
