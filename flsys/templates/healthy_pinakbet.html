Filipino Pinakbet

This Filipino Pinakbet dish is both nutritious and delicious! This famous Filipino stew is a beautiful combination of local vegetables, pork belly, and shrimp paste that works well as a main meal or as a side dish alongside fried fish or grilled meat. This Filipino vegetable meal is one of the simplest to prepare, yet it is delicious and healthy. It does, however, take some cutting. To ensure that all of the vegetables cook equally, cut them all to the same sizes.

From Panlasang Pinoy by Vanjo Merano
Prep. Time

15 min.

Cooking Time

25 min.

Serving

6 Servings

Ingredients

1 lb lechon kawali sliced

1 piece Knorr Shrimp Cube

12 pieces sitaw cut into 2 inch length

1/2 piece kalabasa cubed

12 pieces okra

1 piece Chinese eggplant sliced

1 piece ampalaya sliced

1 piece kamote cubed (optional)

2 pieces tomato cubed

2 thumbs ginger crushed (optional)

1 piece onion chopped

4 cloves garlic crushed

2 teaspoons bagoong alamang

2 ½ cups water

3 tablespoons cooking oil

¼ teaspoon ground black pepper

Instructions

step 1

In a pot, heat the cooking oil. Sauté onion and garlic. Add ginger and continue to cook until the onion softens.

step 2

Add lechon kawali. Saute for 1 minute.

step 3

Pour water. Let boil.

step 4

Add Knorr Shrimp Cube. Cover the pot and cook in medium heat for 20 minutes.

step 5

Add tomato and bagoong alamang. Stir. Cover the pot. Cook for 3 minutes.

step 6

Put the kalabasa and kamote into the pot. Cook for 7 minutes.

step 7

Add sitaw, okra, ampalaya, and eggplant. Stir. Cover the pot and cook for 5 minutes.

step 8

Season with ground black pepper and add remaining lechon kawali. Cook for minutes.

step 9

Transfer to a serving plate. Serve. Share and Enjoy!
