Filipino Spring Rolls (Lumpiang Shanghai)

Lumpia, also known as Lumpiang Shanghai in Filipino, is a Filipino egg roll dish. Ground pork, minced carrots, and minced onion make up the majority of the filler or meat combination. Seasonings and spices are added to the mixture. It is then individually wrapped and rolled in lumpia wrappers, which are thin flour sheets.

From Yummy.ph by Roselle Miranda, July, 25, 2021
Prep. Time

30 min.

Cooking Time

10 min.

Serving

6 servings

Ingredients

2½ lbs. ground pork

1 piece Knorr Pork Cube

50 pieces lumpia wrapper

3/4 cup carrot minced

½ piece garlic minced

2 pieces onions minced

2 pieces eggs

¼ cup parsley minced

½ teaspoon ground black pepper

1 teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon Sesame oil

2 cups cooking oil

Instructions

step 1

In a mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, carrot, garlic, onion, and parsley. Mix well.

step 2

Add the egg, black pepper, salt, and sesame oil. Continue to mix until all of the ingredients are thoroughly combined.

step 3

Crush the Knorr pork cube and add it into the mixture. Combine all of the ingredients completely.

step 4

Put 1 to 1½ tablespoons of meat mixture over a piece of lumpia wrapper, then spread. Wrap and roll to secure the mixture. Continue this step until the mixture is completely consumed.

step 5

In a cooking pot over low to medium heat, heat the oil. The lumpia should be deep fried till golden brown. Remove from the pot and place on a plate with paper towel.

step 6

Transfer to a serving platter. Serve with a sweet and sour sauce on the side. Enjoy and share!
